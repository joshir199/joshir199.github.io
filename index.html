I am an MSc student studying Artificial Intelligence (AI) at Centre for Vision, Speech and Signal Processing (CVSSP), University of Surrey. My research interests involves 3D Computer vision and Large Language models (LLMs) which can be outlined broadly as follows.
  • Developing efficient and robust algorithms for real-time interactive editing of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3D GS) scenes
  • Exploring lightweight and scalable Feed Forward Network (FFN) approaches for pose-free 3D scene reconstruction enabling easy adoption for real-world applications.
  • Designing language-embedded vision systems that integrate large language models with visual understanding for robust multimodal perception.

I have completed my Master's dissertation titled "Object Removal and Recolorization within 3D Gaussian Splatting Scenes" under the supervision of Dr. Jean-Yves Guillemaut. The work introduces a real-time, interactive 3D editing framework that leverages high-quality SAM-HQ masks and learned prior-guided label reassignment to achieve precise object-level manipulation with full multiview consistency. For further details, refer to my dissertation proposals (Reports).

Prior to starting my MSc degree, I was a Lead Engineer at Samsung Research Institute, Bangalore. During my engineering role at Samsung, I led development of notable product features like "Intelligent widgets for SmartHome IoT devices" in the SmartThings app, and "Personalized health insights" for Samsung Health app for Samsung Galaxy flagship phones. Most recently, I worked on efforts to create 3D realistic MapView of SmartHomes through pose-free 3D Gaussian Splatting (3D GS) scene reconstruction, enabling immersive visualization of connected devices. Earlier, I completed my undergraduate in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur.
